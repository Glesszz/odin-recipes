Man-Eating Plant Tart

A salty tart-like dish made from the fruit of different Man-eating plants. A mixture of mashed up and cut Man-eating plant fruit , slime and scorpion soup is poured into a pan lined with the flattened peel of the fruit and cooked before garnishing with some more fruit

Ingredients

(serves 3-4)

  • Leftover scorpion broth (from lunch; 200 ml)
  • Slime gelatin (80 grams)
  • Baraselia (3 med. size)
  • Meeroak (5 or 6 med. size)
  • Betan (3 med. size)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Pepper (a little)

Method

  1. First lightly steam the fruits.
  2. Cut a circle around the stalk, twist it a bit and pull it out. The seeds then should come out cleanly.
  3. Peel the skin from the fruits, hit them and soften it up.
  4. Line the frying pan with the peeled skin.
  5. Mash up the unripe fruit and add some slime and scorpion soup.
  6. Stir until it thickens up.
  7. Once it's smooth, add the rest of the scorpion soup and some roughly chopped fruit.
  8. Mix it a bit, and pour it in the frying pan.
  9. Let it heat up until the surface begins tu bubble.
  10. Garnish it with the last of the fruit.
  11. Enjoy!
